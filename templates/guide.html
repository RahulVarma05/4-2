01 — Input requirements
What you need to upload

There are two inputs: a source image and a driving video. Both have specific requirements for the model to work correctly.

🖼️ Source image requirements
  • ✅ File format: JPG, PNG, or WEBP
  • ✅ Face should fill most of the frame (portrait style)
  • ✅ Face looking straight at the camera (frontal view)
  • ✅ Good, even lighting — no harsh shadows on face
  • ✅ Clear, sharp focus — not blurry
  • ✅ Neutral or natural expression works best
  • ✅ Plain or simple background preferred
  • ❌ Avoid heavy filters, Snapchat/Instagram effects
  • ❌ Avoid extreme side profiles (more than 45° rotation)
  • ❌ Avoid full-body shots where face is tiny
  • ❌ Avoid group photos
🎬 Driving video requirements
  • ✅ File format: MP4, AVI, MOV, or WEBM
  • ✅ Duration: 5–30 seconds (optimal: 10–15 sec)
  • ✅ Face fills most of the 256×256 frame
  • ✅ Shot close-up, like a talking-head video
  • ✅ Natural head movement and facial expressions
  • ✅ Good lighting — face clearly visible
  • ✅ Starting pose should be similar to source image
  • ❌ Avoid wide-angle shots (person is too small)
  • ❌ Avoid multiple people in the video
  • ❌ Avoid very fast motion (blur)
  • ❌ Avoid extreme head rotations past 60°
02 — Examples
Good vs bad source images

The quality and framing of your source image is the single biggest factor in output quality. Here are examples of what works and what does not.

✅ Good source images

  • ✓ Close-up portrait — face fills 60–80% of the frame
  • ✓ Looking straight at camera, head level
  • ✓ Even natural lighting, no strong shadows
  • ✓ Sharp focus, no motion blur
  • ✓ Neutral background (plain wall, studio backdrop)
  • ✓ Mouth closed or slightly open (relaxed expression)
  • ✓ Your source_image.jpg is a great example of this

❌ Bad source images

  • ✗ Full body shot — face is too small for keypoint detection
  • ✗ Extreme side profile — model cannot transfer motion correctly
  • ✗ Group photo — model detects multiple faces and fails
  • ✗ Heavy beauty filter / AI-enhanced skin — distorts features
  • ✗ Blurry, low resolution, or poorly lit face
  • ✗ Sunglasses, mask, or heavy face obstruction
  • ✗ Cartoon or illustrated images (use real photos only)
02b — Driving video examples

Good vs bad driving videos

✅ Good driving videos

  • ✓ Record yourself on your phone, holding it at arm's length
  • ✓ Face fills most of the frame, like a selfie video
  • ✓ Talk normally — natural lip movement and head nods
  • ✓ Good indoor lighting — sit near a window or lamp
  • ✓ Start with a pose similar to the source image (head straight)
  • ✓ News anchor clips, interview footage, talking-head YouTube videos

❌ Bad driving videos

  • ✗ Wide-shot video — person is a tiny figure in the frame
  • ✗ Animated or cartoon driving video
  • ✗ Video with multiple people talking
  • ✗ Very shaky or handheld camera with fast motion
  • ✗ Dark or backlit — face not clearly visible
  • ✗ The ForBiggerFun.mp4 animation file (wrong type entirely)
03 — Step by step
How to use the application

Follow these steps in order for the best results.

1

Prepare your source image

Find a clear, front-facing portrait photo of the person you want to animate. The photo should be well-lit with the face clearly visible and filling most of the frame. Your own photo works perfectly — just like the sample source_image.jpg used in this project.

💡 Tip: If your photo has a complex background, you can use a free tool like remove.bg to clean it up before uploading. A plain background helps the model focus on the face.
2

Prepare your driving video

The driving video provides the motion. The ideal way is to record yourself talking for 10–15 seconds on your phone, held at arm's length with your face filling most of the screen. Good indoor lighting near a window gives the best results. Transfer the video to your computer via USB, WhatsApp, or cloud sync.

💡 Tip: Start the video with a neutral expression looking straight at the camera — this matches the source image pose and prevents a jump at the start of the animation.
3

Upload on the Home page

Go to the Home page. In Step 1, click the source image upload area or drag your photo onto it. You will see a preview appear immediately. In Step 2, either upload your driving video or click "Use sample video" to test with the pre-loaded sample.

💡 Tip: Try with the sample video first before using your own. This lets you verify the tool is working correctly before investing time in recording your own video.
4

Choose your pipeline mode

In Step 3, choose between Animate and Motion Transfer mode. For first-time use, always choose Animate — it is faster, cleaner, and shows you the raw FOMM output. Only switch to Motion Transfer after you have verified Animate works correctly.

5

Click Generate and wait

Click the Generate Video button. The page will show a live progress bar with status messages. Processing takes approximately 2–5 minutes on CPU for a 30-frame clip. Do not close the browser tab while processing — the job runs in the background on the server.

💡 Tip: The max is set to 60 frames (about 5 seconds at 12fps). Longer videos take proportionally more time. For testing, the sample video is already the right length.
6

Preview and download the result

When processing is complete, the result video plays automatically in the browser. Review it to check the motion quality. If you are happy with the output, click "Download MP4" to save the file to your computer. Click "Generate another" to start a new animation.

05 — Output
Understanding your result
📹 Output specifications
  • • Format: MP4 (H.264)
  • • Resolution: 256 × 256 pixels
  • • Frame rate: matches driving video FPS
  • • Max length: 60 frames (about 5 seconds)
  • • No audio in output (add manually if needed)
✅ Signs of good output
  • ✓ Face moves naturally with driving video motion
  • ✓ No dark ring or halo around the face
  • ✓ No jitter or flickering between frames
  • ✓ Source identity preserved (looks like the source photo)
  • ✓ Mouth, eyes, and head move in sync with driving video
06 — Troubleshooting
Common problems and fixes
Output looks almost identical to source image — no motion

The animation is barely visible or the face does not move at all.

Fix: Wrong type of driving video Your driving video is probably a wide shot, animation, or cartoon. The face must be close-up and fill most of the frame. Record yourself talking on your phone or use the sample video.
Face looks distorted or warped incorrectly

The generated face looks stretched, squished, or morphed strangely.

Fix: Pose mismatch at frame 0 The starting pose of the driving video is very different from the source image. Try to record your driving video starting with the same head position as the source photo (looking straight ahead).
Dark ring or halo visible around the face

There is a visible circular border around the composited face area.

Fix: Use Animate mode instead Switch from Motion Transfer to Animate mode — the halo only appears in compositing mode. If you need Motion Transfer, the Poisson blending in our pipeline handles this but requires a good-quality close-up driving video.
Processing takes too long / timeout

The generation never completes or the page shows an error after waiting.

Fix: Shorter driving video Try with a shorter clip (10–15 seconds). The max frames is capped at 60 but longer videos still take more time to load and preprocess. On CPU, expect 2–5 minutes per run.
Skin tone of generated face doesn't match

The face has a different colour or brightness to what was expected.

Fix: Use Motion Transfer mode Switch to Motion Transfer mode which applies per-channel colour correction before blending. This automatically matches the generated face's brightness and colour to the driving video frame.
Model offline — server error

The navbar shows "Model offline" or Generate returns a server error.

Fix: Check checkpoint file The checkpoint file vox-cpk.pth.tar may be missing from the checkpoints/ folder. Run python prepare_demo.py again to re-download it. Check server logs for the exact error.
